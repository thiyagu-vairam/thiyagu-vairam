Payout Dashboard

Replacing Excel with a payment dashboard to drive trust and transparency

About the Project

Seller First by nurture.farm is a platform built to streamline operations for agri-input sellers across India. With ~1,500 registered sellers, including manufacturers and wholesalers, the platform supports a wide network across the country. This project focused on automating monthly payout reports, which were previously shared via Google Sheets—taking 8–10 days for reconciliation.

My Role

I led the end-to-end design of the Seller Payout Automation initiative — starting with field research and conversations with our care team to uncover pain points. I designed the core flows, ran usability tests, and collaborated closely with PMs and business stakeholders to ensure alignment and impact.

Business Objective

Automate Reconciliation

Reduce manual effort and save time with real-time payout visibility.

Boost Platform Engagement

Increase seller stickiness, driving higher GMV.

Improve Seller Experience

Lower payment-related complaints and confusion.

Build Trust & Transparency

Strengthen seller confidence through clear, accurate reports.

Challenges

Business Challenges

Delayed Reconciliation

Monthly reports take 8–10 days to prepare, slowing down financial visibility.

No Platform Visibility

Sellers can’t track payouts directly on SellerFirst—only through emailed sheets.

Manual & Resource-Heavy

Internal teams spend significant time generating and sharing Excel reports.

Confusing Report Format

Dense Excel files with too many fields make it hard for sellers to decode payment info.

Seller Challenges

Cumbersome Excel Reports

Sellers found the monthly payout sheets cluttered, with too many fields and transactions, making them hard to read.

Lack of Order-Payment Mapping

Payments weren’t easily traceable to delivered orders, leading to confusion and follow-ups.

Poor Reconciliation Experience

Comparing payouts across months was manual and error-prone, slowing down internal tracking.

Difficult to Share with Accountants

Reports lacked clarity and structure, making it tough for sellers to use them for tax or audit purposes.

Excel Report Sent to Sellers

Our Focus

How might we make payment reconciliation faster, transparent, and self-serve for sellers?

How might we turn payout tracking into a trust-building experience instead of a transactional one?

Insights Usability Testing

We ran a few usability tests (moderated G-meet), with a figma prototype

Quick Adoption

Sellers welcomed the solution and found the interface intuitive, though some initial guidance was needed.

Clarity & Usability

Detailed payout views and filters improved query resolution, but some users found the filters overwhelming and needed support.

Reporting & Flexibility

Asynchronous report generation for large datasets was praised for smoother access to financial data for audits and tax filing.

Time Savings Real

Real-time access replaced manual reconciliation processes, cutting time from 8–10 days to minutes.

Trust & Transparancy

Clear deduction breakdowns and tax instructions boosted confidence, though simpler tax explanations were requested.

Key Takeaways

User-Centered Approach

Field research and usability testing revealed pain points, ensuring solutions fit real workflows and business goals.

Automation & Efficiency

Automated reconciliation, reducing manual effort and cutting processing time from 8–10 days to real-time.

Simplified Complex Data

Introduced multi-level payout views with contextual details, reducing confusion and improving clarity.

Real-Time Insights

Delivered live account visibility, increasing seller trust and reducing escalations, despite regional network challenges.
