Kisan Shop: A Pilot Initiative

Revolutionising Agri-Input Trade for Indian Farmers & Retailers

About the Project

Nurture.farm operates across three major verticals—.farm, catering to farmers; .retail, serving agri-input retailers; and .partner, enabling rural gig workers to find jobs. To bridge these platforms and create a seamless agricultural commerce ecosystem, we piloted 'Kisan Shop', an integrated solution that connects farmers, retailers, and delivery partners in a single workflow.

My Role

I led the product design for 'Kisan Shop' also contributed to the retailer store creation journey, ensuring that the design effectively translated both business objectives and user needs.

Why - Discovering Opportunities

FARMERS

Physical Dependency

Farmers must visit retailer stores in person to purchase products, which is time-consuming and inconvenient.

Product Authenticity Concerns

There is no guaranteed assurance of genuine products, leading to trust issues.

Limited Pricing Flexibility

Discounts are typically available only for bulk purchases, making it harder for small farmers to benefit.

RETAILERS

Geographical Limitation

Their customer base is restricted to a small radius (a few kilometers), limiting growth potential.

Business Expansion Barriers

Lack of digital presence or scalable channels prevents them from reaching new markets.

Inventory & Pricing Constraints

Fixed pricing models and dependency on local demand reduce competitiveness

Business Objective

Transforming User Behaviour

Disrupt the retailers' selling & farmers' buying behaviour.

Improve Rural Livelihood

Build more opportunities of the rural gig workers

Delivering to Rural Areas

Establish the last mile delivery to the deep most rural villages

To Achieve 30L GMV

4 States, 40 order per day, platform & delivery fees

User Benefits

While common in e-commerce, we ran a quick interest check to ensure it was worth our effort before fully investing.

Farmers

Farmers can order agri-inputs from home, explore more products, compare prices, and enjoy faster, reliable delivery without store visits.

Retailers

Beyond local footfall, retailers can expand their reach to a larger farmer base, boosting sales and revenue with seamless payment settlements.

Partners

More gig job opportunities are created, improving earning potential while offering flexible schedules where partners can choose deliveries that suit their availability.

User Challenges (Discovered via Field Trips)

Farmers

Price-Driven Decisions

Farmers are highly sensitive to price and actively seek discounts.

Trust & Authenticity Concerns

Fear of fake products and fraud creates hesitation.

Varying Digital Confidence

Comfort levels with mobile apps differ based on age and exposure.

Need for Flexible Financing

A strong interest in credit lines like BNPL & Govt. Subsidies.

Retailers

Hesitancy Toward Going Online

Cautious on selling online because fear of pricing exposure.

Inventory & Catalog Complexity

Managing product listings and orders smoothly is a major need.

Digital Adoption Barriers

Traditional retailers show resistance to tech, needing simpler tools.

Customer Support Gaps

Handling buyer queries and complaints - concern for scaling.

Delivery Partners

Pressure from Rural Deliveries

Delivering to remote locations affects time, fuel, and efficiency—especially during peak seasons.

Income Stability is Key

Many seek more consistent delivery tasks to secure a reliable, daily income stream.

Flexibility Matters

They prefer work models that allow juggling other jobs or responsibilities.

Support & Training

Operational clarity and training can reduce errors and improve performance in the field.

Strategic Takeaways

Farmers want convenience

Quick delivery, easy discovery, flexible payments, and return options were top needs — speed and simplicity drive engagement.

Delivery partners seek clarity & control

Accurate locations and instant earnings are essential — especially when handling bulky orders.

Retailers need smarter tools

Simpler onboarding, better leads, inventory help, and payment automation can reduce friction and boost confidence.
